New Algorithm Update! 2-3x Smarter Predictions

What's New? We've upgraded to a smarter, more accurate model tuned specifically to Novi Community Schools and Michigan district norms (based on Superintendent Ben Mainka's guidance, nearby districts like Ann Arbor/Livonia, and National Weather Service frostbite charts).

  • Wind chill is now king — Novi often closes around -20°F wind chill (especially at drop-off/pickup times). New logic: 50% chance at -15°F effective chill, 80% at -20°F, 95%+ at -25°F or below. Uses precise "feels like" data + calculated wind chill.
  • Better ice & frost detection — Detects freezing rain/sleet with weather codes (high risk → big boost), plus frost/black ice from humidity + temp 28–32°F.
  • Smarter precip/road risk — Heavy snow + cold = higher impact (snow worse than rain).
  • Weighted & bonus system — No flat average anymore: wind chill weighs 40%, ice 25%, precip 20%, extreme cold 15%. Extra +20% if multiple bad factors hit (realistic for closures).

Result: Much more realistic chances

  • Wind chill -18°F + light snow → 60–80% (old: ~20–40%)
  • Freezing rain at 30°F → 70–90%
  • Sustained -22°F wind chill → near 90–100%
  • Mild cold (10°F, no extras) → still low (10–30%), matches reality

Predictions are now 2-3x more likely to flag meaningful risks when conditions are borderline-bad — just like real Novi decisions!
